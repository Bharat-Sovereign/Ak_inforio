Executive Summary

The strategic case in brief

Our current multi-cloud environment operates without unified observability, consistent data classification, or a centralised governance layer. Every integration point, every pipeline handoff, every cross-cloud transfer represents potential exposure — regulatory, security, and operational.

This proposal outlines a four-phase architecture built on our existing Atlan investment. It does not require a big-bang migration. Each phase delivers standalone, measurable value — and Phase 1 alone closes our most urgent compliance gap within 90 days.

The architecture addresses three compounding risks simultaneously: DPDP and GDPR regulatory exposure, unprotected cross-cloud data movement, and the absence of lineage-aware alerting that makes root-cause investigation manual and slow.

Key Question

Can we today produce a complete audit trail of where a specific user's data exists across all our cloud systems? If the answer is no — that is our opening compliance gap, and it is provable today.

Three Live Risks

The exposure we carry today, unaddressed

01
Regulatory Risk

DPDP & GDPR Compliance

  • Cannot honour a DPDP erasure request end-to-end — lineage gaps prevent tracing a specific user's data across all systems
  • DPDP penalties reach up to ₹250 crore per breach incident
  • GDPR exposure wherever EU personal data enters our pipelines
  • Consent status disconnected from the data it governs
02
Security Risk

Unprotected Data Movement

  • Confidential fields cross cloud boundaries without tokenisation — raw values exposed at every handoff
  • Per-cloud key management (AWS KMS, GCP KMS) with no unified revocation capability
  • Atlan lineage graph is an unprotected attack surface — maps sensitive data flows to anyone with catalog access
  • A breach could go undetected for weeks with no cross-cloud observability
03
Operational Risk

No Lineage-Aware Operations

  • One upstream failure cascades into dozens of alerts — no automated root-cause tracing
  • Metric definitions inconsistent across clouds — alerts fire but nobody trusts them
  • Shadow pipelines outside governance accumulate as compliance liability
  • Manual incident investigation costs multiply with every new pipeline

Proposed Architecture

Four concentric rings of control

The architecture is structured as four rings, each delivering standalone value. Together they form a complete confidential data governance posture — without replacing existing investments.

Ring One

Classification

Classify at ingestion, automatically. Tags are hereditary via lineage — downstream datasets inherit classification from upstream sources without manual intervention. Three tiers minimum: Internal, Confidential, Restricted. Atlan enforces inheritance across the lineage graph.

Ring Two

Key Management

Centralised key management via HashiCorp Vault — above and across AWS KMS, GCP Cloud KMS, and Azure Key Vault. Envelope encryption as the standard. Tokenisation for all cross-cloud movement of Confidential and Restricted fields: the token travels, the raw value never does.

Ring Three

Zero-Trust Data Mesh

Domain ownership of data products. A central policy broker defines rules; domains enforce them. Every cross-cloud pipeline registered with a formal contract in Atlan declaring source, destination, classification, and purpose. No implicit trust between cloud environments.

Ring Four

Atlan as Control Plane

Elevate Atlan from catalog to policy enforcement point. Access requests evaluated against lineage and classification before approval. Automated compliance checks on every new pipeline registration. Immutable audit trail generated automatically — compliance evidence without manual assembly.

A

Leveraging Your Existing Atlan Investment

Atlan is already deployed. This architecture does not replace it — it extends Atlan into a role we are currently not using: policy enforcement, DPDP erasure workflows, automated classification inheritance, and lineage-aware access control. The catalog becomes the control plane.

Incremental Roadmap

No big-bang migration — each phase funds the next

Phase One
Months 1 – 3

Know What You Have

Audit Atlan lineage coverage. Agree on classification taxonomy. Identify the 20% of datasets representing the highest risk. Deploy HashiCorp Vault.

Deliverable Risk map of highest-exposure data flows. Classification gaps fully documented.
Phase Two
Months 3 – 6

Control What Moves

Pipeline contracts for all cross-cloud movement. Tokenisation for Confidential fields. DPDP consent attributes added as metadata in Atlan. Key management centralised for new pipelines.

Deliverable No new unregistered cross-cloud pipelines. Existing ones flagged for remediation.
Phase Three
Months 6 – 12

Govern Who Touches It

Domain ownership model deployed. Atlan elevated to policy enforcement point. Automated classification inheritance via lineage. DPDP erasure workflow built and operational.

Deliverable All Confidential data access brokered, logged, and auditable end-to-end.
Phase Four
Month 12+

Prove It Continuously

Anomaly detection on cross-cloud access patterns. Automated DPDP compliance reporting. Lineage coverage metrics tracked. Regular red-team exercises targeting the human layer.

Deliverable Audit evidence generated automatically. Compliance posture self-monitored.

Investment & Return

The cost of inaction versus the cost of action

Cost of Inaction

What we continue to carry without this architecture

₹250 crore maximum DPDP penalty per breach incident — escalating as enforcement matures
10–50× multiplier on breach response costs versus prevention — forensics, notification, remediation
Engineering hours lost to manual incident investigation — calculate from your last three major pipeline failures
Procurement risk — governed data posture is increasingly a contractual requirement in regulated industries

Cost of the Architecture

What the programme requires

Phase 1–2: Primarily engineering time and Vault licensing. Low absolute cost, immediate risk reduction.
Phase 3–4: Higher investment, funded by engineering efficiency gains from reduced manual investigation
Atlan is already licensed — we are using an existing asset more fully, not purchasing a new platform
Faster data product launches — governance built in reduces security review cycles on every new pipeline
Bottom Line
The cost of one serious breach — regulatory fine, response costs, and reputational damage — exceeds the total two-year architecture investment by a significant margin. This is risk reduction with a clear and defensible return.
